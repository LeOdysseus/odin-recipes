French Onion Soup

Home

Description

Slowly caramelize heaps of sliced onions in butter and oil until golden and sweet, infused with garlic, thyme, bay, and a pinch of sugar. Deglaze with brandy, stir in beef stock, and simmer into a rich broth. Ladle into bowls, float baguette slices topped with melty Comté cheese, and broil until bubbly. A good winter meal

Ingredients

4 Serving

  • Olive oil
  • 6 large onions
  • 1 clove of gralic
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • French Brandy or Armagnac
  • 1 litre of Beef Broth (3 beef broth cubes) or also work with Chicken
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Croutons
  • Comté cheese or Gruyère

Steps

  1. Melt the butter and olive oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat and add your onions, garlic, thyme and bay leaf.
  2. Mix everything and then lower the heat and put it under a lid.
  3. Leave it to cook for 30 minutes.(Check and mix form time to time)
  4. The onions will cook down and will turn a light golden color.
  5. Cook for another 15 minutes with the lid off. At the same time sprinkle the sugar and mix.
  6. Wait for the onions to turn in a better golden brown color.
  7. Add a generous amount of Brandy or Armagnac. Continue to mix the soup.
  8. Add the Beef (or Chicken) broth, mix it and add the salt, pepper, thyme and bay leaf. Then leave it on the fire for another 10 minutes.
  9. Serve it in bowls and add croutons and grated Comté cheese on top of it.
  10. Enjoy!
